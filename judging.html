Judging Criteria

Teams who fit the following criteria will be eligible to present and win prizes.

Pitch Team Criteria:

Employees can submit a pitch before the main Hack to the Future event. Pitch submissions will be shortlisted by a panel of judges to demo their idea at the Hack kick off event, encouraging Hack teams to work on your idea during Hack to the Future.

  • At least 3 people on the team.
  • Exactly 1 minute to pitch.
  • Submit a completed 1 minute pitch deck and/or video by Wednesday, August 7, 10:00am ET.
  • There will be one prize awarded for Best Overall Pitch.

Hack Team Criteria:

  • At least 4 people from 4 different departments on the team.
  • Must build and present a coded prototype.
  • Submit your hack into one category.
  • Exactly 2 minutes to demo.
  • Submit your team and demo details by Friday, August 9 at 8:00am ET

You can recruit as many people as you like for your team!

Departments are defined as:

  • Content
  • Data Science & Analytics
  • Global Customer Care
  • Marketing
  • Sales Development Representatives (SDR)
  • Finance & Accounting
  • Legal
  • Facilities
  • HR
  • Product
  • User Experience
  • Sales
  • Editorial
  • Platform Solutions
  • Tech (Developers, Engineers, Tech Ops, Infrastructure, QA, IT)
  • Security
  • Delivery

Register your team today on the Pitch registration page or Hack registration page. The deadline for Pitch team registration is Wednesday, August 7 at 11:00am ET, and the deadline for Hack team registration is Friday, August 9 at 8:00am ET.

Judging Categories

Hacks should be focused on improving the experience of customers, contributors or employees at Shutterstock as described below:

  • eCommerce/Self-Service customer experience - hacks that improve the eCommerce platform for customers who want to independently access our content and tools
  • Enterprise customer experience - hacks around improving the features, functionality, and services to customers seeking more support and customized offerings through our high touch channel
  • Platform Solutions/API customer experience - hacks that improve the experience of customers accessing Shutterstock through a third-party platform
  • Contributor experience - hacks that improve an aspect of the contributor experience
  • Internal experience - hacks that create internal efficiencies, enabling employees to be able to do their jobs better
  • Data Commercialization - hacks around leveraging data (e.g. our own, combined with 3rd party) to service customers better or as a new product.

Each submission is only eligible to win in one category. Note: judges may move hacks to a different category they feel is more appropriate.

After Hack demos finish on Friday, August 9, judges will deliberate and then announce the winners via live stream the same day.

Prizes:

The winning teams will receive a prize (details TBD) and their hack/pitch will be considered for a sprint in 2019-20.
